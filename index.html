  • The first step is to take the anti-static bags out of the box and place it on the table I also recommend doing this with the computer. Connect yourself to the Computer and start the teardown.
  • After this you will want to lift the handle and take the side panel off revealing the insides of the computer
  • Here you will see the CD Drive, before you take this out you will want to pull the cables that are connected to it out. After this you will want to pull on the blue tabbed area to remove the CD Drive. To finish this step please place the CD Drive in your anti-static bag.
  • Now we move onto taking the hard drive out of the computer. Start by disconnecting the hard drive by pulling the cables out of it. Simply just lift up on the hard drive to release it from its position, Since were done with this step we can now place it in the anti-static bag and move on.
  • Taking the RAM out is simple, just push the tan things that hold it in place away from the ram and carefully lift them out. Carefully place the RAM in the bag.
  • Lift the latch that is holding the graphics card down. Unplug the item that has a thick red and white wire connected to the actual graphics card. If you look on the side you will see a blue tab holding the graphics card in place, push it away from the card and life the card out carefully.
  • This is the part where we have to put everything back together. First lets start off by putting the graphics card back in place.

Problems

The list of ten common problems to have while taking a computer apart or simply rebuilding one.

  1. Buying incompatible components.
  2. Not doing cable management.
  3. Leaving plastic on parts.
  4. Slotting the RAM wrong.
  5. Plugging monitor into motherboard instead of GPU.
  6. Applying just ther right amount of force.
  7. Trying to plug in the wrong cables.
  8. Careless handling of parts.
  9. Working on it while its plugged in.
  10. Squashing cables.

Steps

Here is how you would fix some of the common issues that many people can run into.

  1. Check on multiple sites for the parts compatibility.
  2. While you are taking putting the computer together make sure you try to hide cables as much as possible.
  3. Make sure that you have all of the thin plastic on top of the parts off.
  4. Look at the slot on the motherboard then on the RAM make sure the indent on the RAM lines up with the part sticking up on the motherboard.
  5. Plug your monitor into your graphics card instead of your motherboard.
  6. To apply just the right amount of force push/pull with only your hands and wrists.
  7. Avoid plugging the wrong cables into the wrong areas make sure that you read the manuals that come with your parts.
  8. Handle your parts carefully, they may look very durable but they have a lot of very small parts on them that allows them to break easily.
